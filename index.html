Why this works

Daily habits of mercy

Three small practices to build your mercy muscle every day.

The Nano-Second Pause

Before reacting to anger or frustration today, force yourself to take one deep breath. That 1-second delay is where mercy is chosen.

The Silent Prayer

When you feel jealousy or resentment toward someone, secretly pray for Allah to give them more. It instantly cleanses your heart of bitterness.

The Secret Act

Do one kind thing today that absolutely no one finds out about. Pick up trash, make a hidden donation, or fix something quietly.

Where this comes from

A simple introduction.

In the Quran, Allah makes a profound promise that serves as the foundation for how He deals with His creation: "He has prescribed for Himself mercy." (Surah Al-An'am 6:12)

And in Islamic tradition, we learn that before the universe was even created, He inscribed this rule above His Throne: "My mercy prevails over My wrath." (Hadith Qudsi, Sahih al-Bukhari 7404)

وَرَحْمَتِي وَسِعَتْ كُلَّ شَيْءٍ
"And My mercy encompasses all things."
Surah Al-A'raf 7:156

This means that compassion, forgiveness, and love come first — before anger or punishment. This is the guiding principle of how Allah runs the universe.

Why this guide exists:

We are humans, and we cannot control the big universe. But we can control the tiny universe inside our own hearts. The goal here is to take that beautiful rule from Allah and apply it to our own everyday choices.

When someone makes you mad, or you make a mistake, you have a split second to react. We call that the nano-second. This guide helps kids and adults alike pause in that exact second and choose mercy — just like Allah does.
